courtney2025

Full citation

Courtney, M. B., Gray, B. P., Schwanke, C. J., Spencer, J. R., & Seitz, A. C. (2025). Insights into the ocean migration, behavior, and ecology of steelhead kelts from Prince of Wales Island, Alaska. Animal Biotelemetry, 13, 8.

DOI
Summary

This study used pop-up satellite archival tags (PSATs) to characterize the post-spawning ocean migration and habitat use of female steelhead kelts originating from three watersheds on Prince of Wales Island in southeast Alaska. Fifteen kelts were tagged in spring 2023, with nine individuals providing usable ocean datasets. Hidden Markov movement models reconstructed westward migrations across the Gulf of Alaska toward the Aleutian Islands and Alaska Peninsula. Tagged steelhead were highly surface-oriented, spending roughly 90% of their time shallower than 4.5 m and occupying waters primarily between 8.7–12.8 °C. Occasional dives exceeded 100 m, but most behavior occurred in near-surface thermal habitats consistent with previous steelhead telemetry studies. The authors conclude that steelhead from southern southeast Alaska share broad migratory pathways and thermal preferences with other North American populations, reinforcing the hypothesis that the western Gulf of Alaska and Aleutian region function as important marine foraging habitat for repeat-spawning steelhead. ohlberger2025

Full citation

Ohlberger, J., Buhle, E. R., Buehrens, T. W., Kendall, N. W., Harbison, T., Claiborne, A. M., Losee, J. P., Whitney, J., & Scheuerell, M. D. (2025). Declining marine survival of steelhead trout linked to climate and ecosystem change. Fish and Fisheries.

DOI
Summary

This paper developed a hierarchical integrated population model (IPM) for multiple Washington coastal steelhead populations to investigate long-term declines in marine survival and abundance. The model incorporated abundance estimates, age composition data, repeat-spawning dynamics, and environmental covariates to estimate recruitment and kelt survival anomalies through time. Results showed widespread declines in both immature steelhead recruitment and post-spawning kelt survival over the past four decades. These declines were strongly associated with increasing summer sea surface temperatures in the North Pacific Ocean, elevated pink salmon abundance, and broad-scale oceanographic variability represented by the North Pacific Gyre Oscillation (NPGO). The analysis suggests that warming marine conditions and intensified interspecific competition in the North Pacific are major drivers of declining steelhead productivity and repeat-spawning rates. The study also highlights the ecological importance of iteroparity in steelhead resilience and demonstrates the utility of hierarchical IPMs for linking marine ecosystem change to salmonid population dynamics.
